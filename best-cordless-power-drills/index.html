Our verdict

No single drill can be perfect for every job, but the Milwaukee 2804-20 comes closest for most people, blending pro-grade power with everyday usability. If you work mainly in wood and want a lighter kit, the DeWalt DCD791D2 is a stellar alternative. Budget buyers should head straight for the Ryobi P251, and anyone tackling heavy concrete or large holes will appreciate the brute force of the DeWalt DCD996B. Whichever you choose, match the tool to your most common task, and you’ll drill smarter, not harder.
